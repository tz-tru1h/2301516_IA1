My Hobby

My strongest trait is discipline, which is deeply tied to my commitment to going to the gym. The routine of consistently working out and pushing myself to meet fitness goals has taught me how to manage my time effectively and prioritize tasks, which directly benefits my academic life.

In school, this discipline translates into a strong work ethic and the ability to focus on completing assignments and preparing for exams. The gym also serves as a stress reliever, allowing me to maintain a balanced mindset during intense study periods. The persistence and resilience I develop in the gym helps me to tackle academic challenges with a positive attitude, knowing that continuous effort leads to growth both physically and mentally.

Personal Motto

It is a philosophy that guides me through, starting in the gym and now applied to all facets of life: "Progress, not perfection." The people who went to the gym were from all levels of their own fitness progressions. Some do heavy weights, others do lighter reps, but the name of the game is always to push more. This formed my perception of self-improvement and later bled into my academic career and my personal struggles.

Whenever things get complicated at school and take more time than what others may be taking, I remind myself that it is not a race about how fast one gets to a point but how much progress one makes towards a particular milestone. That applies during moments of emotional distress, too-even when the recovery is slow, the little steps count in my healing. It's so easy to look around at others who are succeeding-who are moving ahead in their careers or in relationships, perhaps just in personal growth-and feel quite inadequate by comparison. Now I understand that comparing my progress with others was one of the blocks in my mind that didn't let me appreciate my journey.

Life is not about hitting every milestone perfectly at the "right" time. For instance, my father may have achieved more than I had at my age, but that should not debase the growth I have made. The setbacks, mistakes, and even accomplishments irrespective of the size are part of the bigger picture of who I am becoming. This slogan helps me realize that I am always moving toward my goals-even if that path is winding. Most of all, it reminds me not to beat myself up over imperfections but to learn to appreciate the progress I make, however small the stride may be.
